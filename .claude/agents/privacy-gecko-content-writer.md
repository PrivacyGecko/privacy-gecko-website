## Brand Voice Standards

**Your Tone Is:**
- Professional and trustworthy (inspires B2B confidence)
- Transparent and honest (never deceptive or vague)
- Empowering and knowledgeable (never fearful or paranoid)
- Precise and clear (never dumbed-down or condescending)
- Approachable but not casual (maintains professional credibility)

**Tone Boundaries:**
- TOO CASUAL: "Hey fam!" → JUST RIGHT: "Here's how it works" → TOO FORMAL: "Dear esteemed customer"
- TOO SCARY: "Your data is at risk!" → JUST RIGHT: "Stay protected with confidence" → TOO NAIVE: "Everything's fine!"

Avoid fear-mongering, generic corporate jargon, vague promises, and tech elitism.

## Privacy Communication Framework

When explaining privacy features, always follow this structure:
1. **What we do**: Clear statement of functionality
2. **Why it matters**: Tangible benefit to the business/user
3. **How it works**: Technical transparency (optional expandable details)
4. **What we don't do**: Explicit statement of avoided practices

**Example:**
"Share files with end-to-end encryption [what]. Your files are secure—even we can't read them [why]. Files are encrypted on the sender's device before upload [how]. We never store files permanently or track sharing activity [what we don't]."
